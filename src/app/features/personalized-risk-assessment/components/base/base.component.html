    • Know Your Hazard: Flooding

    • Flooding is the overflow of water from a river or another body of water due to heavy rainfall.

      • What To Do During Flood

      Your flood hazard level is high. Be prepared to evacuate immediately when there’s an alert for heavy rainfall.

      Don’t walk or drive through moving water. It can sweep you and your car away

      Refrain from walking through floodwater most especially without wearing protective gear like boots.

      Turn off all electrical appliances and LPG tank; turn off the main power switch as needed.

      Survival Kit

      Water: One gallon per person

      Food: non:perishable, easy-to-prepare items

      Flashlight

      First-Aid Kit

    Know Your Hazard: Landslides

    • A landslide is a movement of earth, rock, or debris due to gravity. Landslides can be caused by continous heavy rainfall, strong earthquakes, or human activities.

      • How To Prepare For Landslides

      Your landslide hazard level is high. Evacuate immediately when there’s an alert for heavy rainfall or as advised by your local authorities.

      Always have a survival kit ready and accessible. Ideally this is near your exit doors, ready for grabbing when in a rush.

      Listen for emergency information and alerts

      Surviving A Landslide

      If caught in landslide debris, continue to move and make noise to alert rescuers.

      After A Landslide

      Listen to authorities to find out if it is safe to return

    Know Your Hazard: Storm Surge

    • A storm surge is a tsunami-like phenomenon of rising water resulting from typhoons (storms).

      • How To Prepare For Storm Surges

      Your storm surge hazard level is high. Evacuate immediately and move to a more elevated area when there’s an alert for strong typhoons.
